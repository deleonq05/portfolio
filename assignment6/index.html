There's this place...

There's this place called Tagaytay. It's a city in the province of Cavite in the Philippines. Through a number of viewpoint sites located across the city, Tagaytay offers views of Taal Volcano Island in the center of Taal Lake in Batangas. In reference to the Tagaytay Ridge, where the city is located, the Tagalog terms tagaytay or taytay, which mean "mountain ridge" or "low mountain range," are the source of the name Tagaytay. It's beautiful, it really is. It's one of the places in the Philippines where you can find a lighter breeze and many shades from the unrelentless, beating sun. It's a sanctuary for me and my family.

I dream of it some days...

I dream of it some days. When I'm deep in sleep, tucked away in my bed, I often feel the sense of light precipitation. Light droplets falling around me, lulling me deeper into sleep. I dream of sweet mangosteen and ripe mangoes, my finger stained and sticky with the juices. I can just hear my mom next to me, telling me what differentiates good fruit to bad fruit. I still vex her with the same response, "Mom, I don't understand the difference." Even in sleep I can imagine the smell, my brain tells me it smells like car exhaust and grilled chicken intenstines. It smells like pool chlorine and freshly cut grass. My hand feels the grasp of family members, their calloused hands grips mine. I don't want to let go, but I do. When I wake up, I crave of sweet fruits and long for mountains sights.
